Chess: The Battle of Kings

World Chess Championship

The World Chess Championship is a match between the current World Chess Champion and a challenger. This year is an exception as the current world champion, Magnus Carlsen, has decided not to defend his title. The winner of the match is awarded the title of World Chess Champion. The championship match is held every two years, with the exception of 2020, which was cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The World Chess Championship is organized by FIDE, the World Chess Federation.

We hope you have been enjoying the World Chess Championship games so far. We wanted to inform you that the latest games have resulted in a tie between the players. Therefore, we will now be moving onto the tiebreaker round to determine the winner of this year's championship.

The tiebreaker round will consist of a series of rapid games, where the players will have less time to make their moves. This will add an exciting new dimension to the competition and we are looking forward to seeing how it plays out.

Stay tuned for more updates on the schedule and format of the tiebreaker round. Thank you for your continued support of the World Chess Championship.
